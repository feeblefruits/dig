How to crack Africa's biggest lawsuit

It took more than a century of commissions, investigations, litigations, acts and reports for the industry to agree to compensate gold miners and their dependents who were affected by silicosis and tuberculosis. But one of the key drivers that has led to the R5-billion compensation agreement have been the advances in machine learning and clever process hacks.
Today the use of technology have fundamentally changed the way large class action lawsuits are being done. Key tasks that would otherwise have taken years for a group of paralegals to complete can now be processed and analysed in a matter of weeks.
